Know the Test
How the SSAT Works
▼

Upper Level. 5 scored sections + 1 unscored writing sample. About 3 hours total.

📋 The Sections (in order)

  1. Writing Sample — 25 min. Pick one of two prompts, write an essay. Not scored, but GAC will see it.
  2. ⏱ 10-minute break — snack, restroom
  3. Quantitative (Math) 1 — 30 min, 25 questions
  4. Reading Comprehension — 40 min, 40 questions
  5. ⏱ 10-minute break
  6. Verbal — 30 min, 60 questions. Synonyms (30) + analogies (30).
  7. Quantitative (Math) 2 — 30 min, 25 questions
  8. Experimental — 15 min, 16 questions. Not scored. Don't stress.

🎯 Scoring

  1. Each scored section: 440–710 points. Total score range: 1320–2130.
  2. +1 point for each correct answer.
  3. −¼ point for each wrong answer. Blank = no penalty.
  4. Your percentile is what matters most — it compares you to other test-takers. GAC looks for above 50th percentile.

Test Day Strategy
▼

Smart play beats hard play. These rules are worth points.

🧠 The 4 Rules

  1. Eliminate then guess. If you can cross off even 1 answer, guess from the rest. If you have zero clue, skip it — the ¼-point penalty means random guessing hurts you.
  2. Don't get stuck. Every question is worth the same. If one is taking too long, mark it, skip, come back. Keep moving.
  3. Easy ones first. Skim the section, answer what you know, then go back for the harder ones. Bank those points.
  4. Watch the clock. Verbal is fast — 60 questions in 30 minutes = 30 seconds each. Don't overthink synonyms, go with your gut.

✍️ Writing Sample Tips

  1. Pick the prompt you have a real opinion on. Don't pick the "harder" one to impress anyone.
  2. Intro → 2-3 body paragraphs with examples → conclusion. Keep it organized.
  3. Schools see this. Write in your own voice, not like a robot. Be clear, not fancy.

🏁 Morning Of

  1. Eat a real breakfast. Not just a granola bar.
  2. Bring: admission ticket (printed or on phone), 3 sharpened #2 pencils with good erasers, snacks in a clear bag, drink in a clear bottle with your name on it.
  3. Don't bring: watches (none allowed — clocks are in the room), calculator, backpack, mechanical pencils, pens, or notes. Phone must be OFF and goes in a zip bag at the door.
  4. Arrive 30-45 min early. Late = no entry once testing starts.
  5. You've done the work. Stay calm, trust your prep.

Word Problems

SSAT math loves word problems. These are the 5 topics you missed on your practice test. Each answer shows you how to read the question, connect the words to the math, and solve it.

Problem 1 Slope
A hiker starts at an elevation of 200 feet. After walking 4 miles, he is at 600 feet. What is his rate of elevation change per mile?
(A) 50 ft/mile   (B) 100 ft/mile   (C) 150 ft/mile   (D) 200 ft/mile   (E) 400 ft/mile
Topic: Slope — Rate of Change Reading the question: "Rate of elevation change per mile" = slope. "Starts at 200 feet" = first y-value. "After 4 miles, at 600 feet" = second point. Miles = x, elevation = y. Two points: (0, 200) and (4, 600) Slope = (600 − 200) / (4 − 0) = 400 / 4 = 100 In plain English: He climbed 400 feet over 4 miles. Divide the total climb by the total distance to get the rate — 100 feet per mile. Answer: (B) 100 ft/mile
Problem 2 Slope
A pool is being drained. It starts with 1,200 gallons and loses 150 gallons per hour. Which equation represents the amount of water w after h hours?
(A) w = 150h + 1200   (B) w = 1200 − 150h   (C) w = 1200h − 150   (D) w = 150 − 1200h   (E) w = 1200 + 150h
Topic: Slope-Intercept Form (y = mx + b) Reading the question: "1,200 gallons" = starting amount = b. "Loses 150 gallons per hour" = rate = m = −150 (negative because it's going down). "After h hours" = the variable. w = −150h + 1200, or equivalently w = 1200 − 150h In plain English: You start with 1,200 and subtract 150 every hour. "Loses," "drains," "decreases" → the slope is negative. Answer: (B) w = 1200 − 150h
Problem 3 Ratios
A recipe calls for 3 cups of flour for every 2 cups of sugar. If a baker uses 12 cups of flour, how many cups of sugar does she need?
(A) 6   (B) 8   (C) 9   (D) 10   (E) 18
Topic: Proportions — Cross Multiplication Reading the question: "3 cups of flour for every 2 cups of sugar" = ratio of flour to sugar = 3/2. "12 cups of flour" = the new flour amount. Sugar = x. Set up the proportion: 3/2 = 12/x Cross multiply: 3x = 24 Divide: x = 8 In plain English: If 3 cups of flour goes with 2 cups of sugar, then 12 cups of flour (which is 4 times as much) needs 4 × 2 = 8 cups of sugar. Proportions just scale the recipe up or down. Answer: (B) 8
Problem 4 Percentages
Jackson scored 27 points in a basketball game. His team scored 90 points total. What percentage of the team's points did Jackson score?
(A) 25%   (B) 27%   (C) 30%   (D) 33%   (E) 35%
Topic: Percentages Reading the question: "27 points" = the part. "90 points total" = the whole. "What percentage" = (part / whole) × 100. 27 / 90 = 0.3 0.3 × 100 = 30% In plain English: What fraction of 90 is 27? Divide 27 by 90. You get 0.3, which is 30 out of 100, which is 30%. Shortcut: 27/90 → both divisible by 9 → 3/10 = 30%. Answer: (C) 30%
Problem 5 Probability
A spinner has 8 equal sections: 3 red, 2 blue, 2 green, and 1 yellow. What is the probability of NOT landing on red?
(A) 3/8   (B) 1/2   (C) 5/8   (D) 3/5   (E) 7/8
Topic: Probability — Complement ("NOT") Reading the question: "NOT landing on red" = complement. First find P(red), then subtract from 1. P(red) = 3/8 P(NOT red) = 1 − 3/8 = 8/8 − 3/8 = 5/8 In plain English: 3 out of 8 slices are red. That means 5 out of 8 are NOT red. When the question says "NOT," use the complement: subtract the probability from 1. Answer: (C) 5/8
Problem 6 Coordinate Geometry
Two corners of a rectangular field are at (1, 3) and (7, 11). What is the length of the diagonal of the field?
(A) 6   (B) 8   (C) 10   (D) 12   (E) 14
Topic: Distance Formula Reading the question: "Two corners" = two points. "Length of the diagonal" = distance between them. Use the distance formula. Horizontal gap: 7 − 1 = 6 Vertical gap: 11 − 3 = 8 d = √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 In plain English: The diagonal of this rectangle makes a right triangle with legs of 6 and 8. That's the 6-8-10 Pythagorean triple — the answer is 10 without any heavy calculation. Answer: (C) 10
Problem 7 Factoring
The area of a square garden is (x² − 16) square meters. Which expression represents the length of one side?
(A) (x − 4)   (B) (x + 4)   (C) (x − 8)   (D) (x − 2)   (E) (x + 16)
Topic: Factoring — Difference of Squares Reading the question: "Area of a square" = side × side. "x² − 16" is a difference of squares since x² and 16 (= 4²) are both perfect squares. x² − 16 = (x + 4)(x − 4) Since a square has equal sides, and the area factors into (x+4)(x−4), each side must be one of these factors. Since a length must be positive, and both factors are used: one side = (x − 4), as long as x > 4. In plain English: Recognize the pattern — something squared minus a perfect square number. It always splits into (something + root)(something − root). Answer: (A) (x − 4)
Problem 8 Ratios
A car travels 240 miles on 8 gallons of gas. At the same rate, how many gallons are needed to travel 420 miles?
(A) 12   (B) 14   (C) 16   (D) 18   (E) 20
Topic: Rates & Proportions Reading the question: "240 miles on 8 gallons" = a rate. "At the same rate" = set up a proportion. "How many gallons for 420 miles" = solve for the unknown. Step 1: Find the rate. 240 ÷ 8 = 30 miles per gallon. Step 2: Use the rate. 420 ÷ 30 = 14 gallons. Or set up the proportion: 240/8 = 420/x Cross multiply: 240x = 3360 x = 3360 ÷ 240 = 14 In plain English: The car gets 30 miles per gallon. To go 420 miles, divide 420 by 30. Both methods — unit rate or proportion — give you the same answer. Answer: (B) 14
Problem 9 Probability
A bag contains 5 red marbles, 3 blue marbles, and 2 green marbles. If Marcus picks one marble and then, without replacing it, picks a second marble, what is the probability that both marbles are red?
(A) 1/4   (B) 2/9   (C) 1/5   (D) 25/100   (E) 5/10
Topic: Probability — Dependent Events ("without replacing") Reading the question: "Without replacing" is the key phrase. After the first pick, the total and the count change. This is dependent probability — multiply, but update the numbers after the first pick. First pick: P(red) = 5/10 = 1/2 After removing 1 red: 4 red left, 9 total left. Second pick: P(red) = 4/9 P(both red) = 1/2 × 4/9 = 4/18 = 2/9 In plain English: The first pick is 5 out of 10. But now there are only 9 marbles left and only 4 red ones. Multiply the two probabilities. "Without replacing" always means the second probability changes. Answer: (B) 2/9
Problem 10 Coordinate Geometry
Point A is at (−2, 5) and Point B is at (6, 1). What are the coordinates of the midpoint of segment AB?
(A) (4, 6)   (B) (2, 3)   (C) (4, 3)   (D) (2, 6)   (E) (8, 4)
Topic: Midpoint Formula Reading the question: "Midpoint of segment AB" = the point exactly halfway between A and B. Average the x's, average the y's. x: (−2 + 6) / 2 = 4 / 2 = 2 y: (5 + 1) / 2 = 6 / 2 = 3 Midpoint = (2, 3) In plain English: Add the two x-coordinates and divide by 2. Do the same for y. Watch the negative sign — −2 + 6 = 4, not 8. Answer: (B) (2, 3)
Problem 11 Exponents
Simplify: (4x4 · 2x3)2
(A) 8x7   (B) 64x14   (C) 8x14   (D) 64x7   (E) 16x12
Topic: Exponent Rules — Multiply Then Power Reading the question: Two rules at work here. First, multiply the terms inside the brackets. Then, raise the result to the power outside. Two steps, don't rush. Step 1: Multiply inside the brackets. (4x4)(2x3) Coefficients: 4 × 2 = 8 Variables: x4 × x3 = x7 (add exponents when bases match) Inside simplifies to: 8x7 Step 2: Apply the outside exponent. (8x7)2 Coefficient: 82 = 64 Variable: (x7)2 = x14 (multiply exponents when raising a power to a power) Result: 64x14 In plain English: First combine what's inside the brackets — multiply the numbers and add the exponents. Then square the whole thing — square the number and double the exponent. Two separate steps. Answer: (B) 64x14
Problem 12 Factoring
Factor completely: 3x2 − 12
(A) 3(x − 4)(x + 1)   (B) 3(x + 2)(x − 2)   (C) (3x + 6)(x − 2)   (D) 3(x − 2)2   (E) (x + 2)(3x − 6)
Topic: Factoring — GCF + Difference of Squares Reading the question: "Factor completely" means pull out everything you can — don't stop after one step. This problem has two layers. Step 1: Pull out the GCF (greatest common factor). Both 3x2 and 12 are divisible by 3. 3x2 − 12 = 3(x2 − 4) Step 2: Recognize the difference of squares. x2 − 4 = x2 − 22 = (x + 2)(x − 2) Step 3: Put it all together. 3(x + 2)(x − 2) In plain English: First pull out the 3 that both terms share. Then you're left with x2 − 4, which is a difference of squares (x squared minus 2 squared). "Completely" is the key word — GCF first, then keep going. Answer: (B) 3(x + 2)(x − 2)
Problem 13 Factoring
Factor: x2 + 5x − 24
(A) (x + 8)(x − 3)   (B) (x − 8)(x + 3)   (C) (x + 6)(x − 4)   (D) (x + 12)(x − 2)   (E) (x − 6)(x + 4)
Topic: Factoring Trinomials Reading the question: x2 + 5x − 24 is a trinomial (three terms). Find two numbers that multiply to −24 and add to +5. Step 1: List factor pairs of −24. Because the product is negative, one number is positive and one is negative: 8 × (−3) = −24    and    8 + (−3) = 5 ✓ Step 2: Write the factored form. x2 + 5x − 24 = (x + 8)(x − 3) Step 3: Check with FOIL. x·x = x2,   x·(−3) = −3x,   8·x = 8x,   8·(−3) = −24 x2 + 8x − 3x − 24 = x2 + 5x − 24 ✓ In plain English: When the last number is negative, your two factors have different signs. Try pairs until one combo adds to the middle number. Always FOIL to double-check. Answer: (A) (x + 8)(x − 3)
Problem 14 Exponents
Simplify: (5x5)(3x3)2
(A) 15x8   (B) 45x11   (C) 15x15   (D) 45x30   (E) 225x11
Topic: Exponent Rules — Order of Operations Reading the question: The squared only applies to (3x3), not to the whole thing. Exponents before multiplication — handle the power first, then multiply. Step 1: Apply the exponent to (3x3)2. The exponent hits every piece inside the parentheses: 32 = 9    and    (x3)2 = x6 (multiply exponents: 3 × 2 = 6) So (3x3)2 = 9x6 Step 2: Multiply (5x5)(9x6). Coefficients: 5 × 9 = 45 Variables: x5 × x6 = x11 (add exponents: 5 + 6 = 11) Result: 45x11 In plain English: The squared only touches what it's next to — (3x3). Square that piece first, then multiply the two terms together. Common trap: choice (A) is what you get if you skip the squaring step and just multiply 5 × 3 and add 5 + 3. Answer: (B) 45x11
Problem 15 Slope
A skateboard ramp rises 6 feet over a horizontal distance of 8 feet. What is the slope of the ramp?
(A) 3/4   (B) 4/3   (C) 6/14   (D) 8/6   (E) 2/3
Topic: Slope — Rise Over Run Reading the question: "Rises 6 feet" = the rise (vertical change). "Horizontal distance of 8 feet" = the run. Slope = rise / run. Slope = 6 / 8 = 3/4 In plain English: The ramp goes up 6 for every 8 across. Simplify that fraction — both divide by 2 — and you get 3/4. Always simplify if you can, because the answer choices usually show the reduced form. Answer: (A) 3/4
Problem 16 Slope
A line passes through the points (2, −1) and (6, 11). What is the slope of the line?
(A) 2   (B) 3   (C) 4   (D) 5   (E) 6
Topic: Slope Formula Reading the question: Two points: (2, −1) and (6, 11). Use the slope formula: m = (y&sub2; − y&sub1;) / (x&sub2; − x&sub1;). m = (11 − (−1)) / (6 − 2) m = 12 / 4 m = 3 In plain English: The y-values go from −1 to 11 — that's a rise of 12. The x-values go from 2 to 6 — that's a run of 4. 12 divided by 4 is 3. Watch the sign: subtracting a negative (−(−1)) becomes adding (+1), so 11 + 1 = 12. Answer: (B) 3
Problem 17 Slope
The table shows the cost of renting a bike: Hours (x): 1, 3, 5, 7 Cost (y): $14, $26, $38, $50 What is the hourly rate, and how much is the initial rental fee?
(A) $6/hr, $8 fee   (B) $8/hr, $6 fee   (C) $7/hr, $7 fee   (D) $12/hr, $2 fee   (E) $6/hr, $14 fee
Topic: Slope-Intercept from a Table Reading the question: "Hourly rate" = slope (how much cost changes per hour). "Initial rental fee" = y-intercept (the cost when hours = 0). Pick any two rows from the table and find the slope. Step 1: Find the slope (rate). Using (1, 14) and (3, 26): m = (26 − 14) / (3 − 1) = 12 / 2 = $6 per hour Step 2: Find the y-intercept (fee). Use y = mx + b with one point. Plug in (1, 14): 14 = 6(1) + b 14 = 6 + b b = 8 So the equation is y = 6x + 8. The rate is $6/hr and the up-front fee is $8. Quick check: x = 5: 6(5) + 8 = 38 ✓   x = 7: 6(7) + 8 = 50 ✓ In plain English: The cost goes up $12 every 2 hours, so that's $6 per hour. Plug back in to find the starting fee. Choice (E) is the trap — $14 is the cost at hour 1, not the fee at hour 0. Answer: (A) $6/hr, $8 fee
Problem 18 Slope
Line A has a slope of 2/5. Line B is perpendicular to Line A. What is the slope of Line B?
(A) 2/5   (B) −2/5   (C) 5/2   (D) −5/2   (E) −5/7
Topic: Perpendicular Slopes — Negative Reciprocals Reading the question: "Perpendicular" = the two lines cross at a 90° angle. Perpendicular slopes are always negative reciprocals of each other. Step 1: Flip the fraction. 2/5 → 5/2 Step 2: Change the sign. 5/2 → −5/2 In plain English: Two steps — flip it and negate it. If the original slope is positive, the perpendicular slope is negative (and vice versa). Choice (B) is the trap — that's just the negative, without flipping. Choice (C) is the other trap — flipped but not negated. You need both. Answer: (D) −5/2
Problem 19 Slope
A candle is 18 inches tall and burns at a constant rate. After 3 hours it is 12 inches tall. How tall will it be after 7 hours?
(A) 2 in   (B) 4 in   (C) 6 in   (D) 8 in   (E) 10 in
Topic: Slope — Linear Equation from Context Reading the question: "18 inches tall" = starting height (b = 18). "After 3 hours, 12 inches" = a second data point. "Constant rate" = linear. "How tall after 7 hours" = plug in x = 7. Step 1: Find the rate (slope). Change in height: 12 − 18 = −6 Change in time: 3 − 0 = 3 Rate = −6 / 3 = −2 inches per hour Step 2: Write the equation. y = −2x + 18 Step 3: Plug in x = 7. y = −2(7) + 18 = −14 + 18 = 4 In plain English: The candle loses 2 inches every hour. After 7 hours that's 14 inches burned off. 18 − 14 = 4 inches left. "Burns at a constant rate" is the keyword that tells you it's linear — same amount lost each hour. Answer: (B) 4 in
Problem 20 Algebra Word Problem
Marcus has $5.00. He buys 3 packs of gum at $0.75 each and a drink for $1.50. How much change does he get?
(A) $0.75   (B) $1.00   (C) $1.25   (D) $1.50   (E) $2.25
Topic: Classic Problem Solving Reading the question: Total spent = 3 × $0.75 + $1.50. Change = $5.00 − total. 3 × $0.75 = $2.25 $2.25 + $1.50 = $3.75 $5.00 − $3.75 = $1.25 Answer: (C) $1.25
Problem 21 Functions
If f(x) = 3x − 7, what is f(5)?
(A) 2   (B) 8   (C) 10   (D) 15   (E) 22
Topic: Functions Reading the question: f(5) means plug in 5 wherever you see x. f(5) = 3(5) − 7 = 15 − 7 = 8 In plain English: "f(5)" is just shorthand for "replace x with 5 and calculate." Answer: (B) 8
Problem 22 Graphs
A line passes through the origin and the point (4, 12). Which of the following points is also on this line?
(A) (1, 4)   (B) (2, 6)   (C) (3, 8)   (D) (5, 14)   (E) (6, 16)
Topic: Graphs Reading the question: "Through the origin" = (0, 0). Find the slope first, then check which point fits. Slope = (12 − 0) / (4 − 0) = 3. So y = 3x. Check (2, 6): 3 × 2 = 6 ✓ Answer: (B) (2, 6)
Problem 23 Linear Equations
If 2x + 3y = 18 and x = 3, what is y?
(A) 2   (B) 3   (C) 4   (D) 5   (E) 6
Topic: Linear Equations / Systems Reading the question: Plug in x = 3 and solve for y. 2(3) + 3y = 18 6 + 3y = 18 3y = 12 y = 4 Answer: (C) 4
Problem 24 Polynomials
Simplify: (2x + 3)(x − 4)
(A) 2x2 − 5x − 12   (B) 2x2 + 5x − 12   (C) 2x2 − 8x − 12   (D) 2x2 − 5x + 12   (E) 2x2 + 11x − 12
Topic: Polynomial Expressions (FOIL) FOIL: First: 2x × x = 2x2 Outer: 2x × (−4) = −8x Inner: 3 × x = 3x Last: 3 × (−4) = −12 Combine: 2x2 − 8x + 3x − 12 = 2x2 − 5x − 12 Answer: (A) 2x2 − 5x − 12
Problem 25 Quadratic Equations
What are the solutions to x2 − 9 = 0?
(A) 3 only   (B) −3 only   (C) 3 and −3   (D) 9 and −9   (E) 0 and 9
Topic: Quadratic Equations x2 − 9 = 0 x2 = 9 x = ±3 Or factor as difference of squares: (x + 3)(x − 3) = 0, so x = 3 or x = −3. Answer: (C) 3 and −3
Problem 26 Radical Expressions
Simplify: √50 + √18
(A) √68   (B) 8√2   (C) 4√17   (D) 6√2   (E) 5√2 + 3√2
Topic: Radical Expressions Simplify each first: √50 = √(25 × 2) = 5√2 √18 = √(9 × 2) = 3√2 Now add (same radical): 5√2 + 3√2 = 8√2 Trap: (A) is wrong because you can't add numbers under the radical sign. Answer: (B) 8√2
Problem 27 Rational Expressions
Simplify: (6x3) / (2x)
(A) 3x   (B) 4x2   (C) 3x2   (D) 3x3   (E) 6x2
Topic: Rational Expressions Divide the coefficients: 6 ÷ 2 = 3 Subtract the exponents: x3 ÷ x1 = x2 Result: 3x2 Answer: (C) 3x2
Problem 28 Volume
A box is 8 cm long, 5 cm wide, and 3 cm tall. What is its volume?
(A) 16 cm³   (B) 40 cm³   (C) 80 cm³   (D) 120 cm³   (E) 240 cm³
Topic: 3D — Volume / Surface Area Volume = length × width × height V = 8 × 5 × 3 = 120 cm³ Answer: (D) 120 cm³
Problem 29 Angles
Two angles are supplementary. One angle is 65°. What is the other?
(A) 25°   (B) 65°   (C) 90°   (D) 115°   (E) 125°
Topic: Angles Supplementary angles add up to 180°. 180 − 65 = 115° Complementary = 90°. Supplementary = 180°. Don't mix them up. Answer: (D) 115°
Problem 30 Area
A circle has a radius of 7 cm. What is its area? (Use π ≈ 22/7)
(A) 44 cm2   (B) 77 cm2   (C) 154 cm2   (D) 196 cm2   (E) 308 cm2
Topic: Area Area = πr2 = (22/7) × 72 = (22/7) × 49 = 22 × 7 = 154 cm2 When they give you π = 22/7, use it — the 7's cancel out nicely. Answer: (C) 154 cm2
Problem 31 Unit Conversion
A runner completes a 5-kilometer race. How many meters did she run?
(A) 50 m   (B) 500 m   (C) 5,000 m   (D) 50,000 m   (E) 500,000 m
Topic: Converting Units / Dimensional Analysis 1 kilometer = 1,000 meters 5 km × 1,000 = 5,000 meters Answer: (C) 5,000 m
Problem 32 Perimeter
A rectangle has a length of 12 and a width of 5. What is its perimeter?
(A) 17   (B) 24   (C) 34   (D) 60   (E) 120
Topic: Length / Perimeter / Circumference Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2(12 + 5) = 2(17) = 34 Trap: (A) is just length + width without doubling. (D) is the area. Answer: (C) 34
Problem 33 Pythagorean Theorem
A right triangle has legs of length 5 and 12. What is the hypotenuse?
(A) 7   (B) 10   (C) 13   (D) 15   (E) 17
Topic: Pythagorean Theorem a2 + b2 = c2 52 + 122 = 25 + 144 = 169 c = √169 = 13 Shortcut: 5-12-13 is a Pythagorean triple. Memorize it alongside 3-4-5 and 8-15-17. Answer: (C) 13
Problem 34 Transformations
Point A is at (3, 2). If it is reflected across the y-axis, what are its new coordinates?
(A) (−3, 2)   (B) (3, −2)   (C) (−3, −2)   (D) (2, 3)   (E) (−2, 3)
Topic: Transformations Reflecting across the y-axis flips the x-coordinate's sign. The y stays the same. (3, 2) → (−3, 2) Rules: • Reflect over y-axis: (x, y) → (−x, y) • Reflect over x-axis: (x, y) → (x, −y) Answer: (A) (−3, 2)
Problem 35 Counting
A restaurant offers 4 appetizers, 6 main courses, and 3 desserts. How many different 3-course meals (one of each) can be ordered?
(A) 13   (B) 24   (C) 36   (D) 48   (E) 72
Topic: Counting Multiply the choices at each step: 4 × 6 × 3 = 72 Trap: (A) adds them (4+6+3=13). When choices are independent, you multiply. Answer: (E) 72
Problem 36 Data & Tables
A class survey shows: Basketball 12 students, Soccer 8, Tennis 5, Swimming 7. What fraction of the class chose Basketball?
(A) 3/8   (B) 1/3   (C) 12/27   (D) 12/32   (E) 1/4
Topic: Data Graphs & Tables Total students: 12 + 8 + 5 + 7 = 32 Basketball: 12/32 = 3/8 Answer: (A) 3/8
Problem 37 Mean / Median / Mode
Jackson scored 18, 22, 25, 22, and 13 points in 5 games. What is the median?
(A) 18   (B) 20   (C) 22   (D) 25   (E) 13
Topic: Mean / Median / Mode / Range Order them: 13, 18, 22, 22, 25 Median = the middle number = 22 Mean = (13+18+22+22+25)/5 = 100/5 = 20. Mode = 22 (most frequent). Range = 25−13 = 12. Answer: (C) 22
Problem 38 Set Theory
In a class of 30, 18 play basketball and 14 play soccer. If 6 play both, how many play neither?
(A) 2   (B) 4   (C) 6   (D) 8   (E) 12
Topic: Set Theory Use the formula: |A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| − |Both| Players in at least one sport: 18 + 14 − 6 = 26 Neither: 30 − 26 = 4 The "minus both" step prevents double-counting kids who play both sports. Answer: (B) 4
Problem 39 Arithmetic
What is 3/4 + 2/3?
(A) 5/7   (B) 5/12   (C) 17/12   (D) 1   (E) 6/7
Topic: Arithmetic Find a common denominator. LCD of 4 and 3 = 12. 3/4 = 9/12 2/3 = 8/12 9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 (or 1 5/12) Trap: (A) just adds tops and bottoms. Never do that with fractions. Answer: (C) 17/12
Problem 40 Estimation
Which is closest to 398 × 21?
(A) 4,000   (B) 6,000   (C) 8,000   (D) 10,000   (E) 12,000
Topic: Estimation Round: 398 ≈ 400, and 21 ≈ 20. 400 × 20 = 8,000. Actual: 398 × 21 = 8,358. Closest is 8,000. On the SSAT, estimation questions reward rounding smartly, not calculating exactly. Answer: (C) 8,000
Problem 41 Number Theory
What is the greatest common factor (GCF) of 36 and 48?
(A) 4   (B) 6   (C) 8   (D) 12   (E) 24
Topic: Number Sense / Number Theory Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36 Factors of 48: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 48 Biggest number in both lists: 12. Shortcut: prime factor both. 36 = 22 × 32. 48 = 24 × 3. Take the smaller power of each: 22 × 3 = 12. Answer: (D) 12
Problem 42 Order of Operations
Evaluate: 3 + 4 × 22 − 1
(A) 18   (B) 27   (C) 14   (D) 24   (E) 15
Topic: Order of Operations (PEMDAS) Step 1: Exponents. 22 = 4 Expression becomes: 3 + 4 × 4 − 1 Step 2: Multiplication. 4 × 4 = 16 Expression becomes: 3 + 16 − 1 Step 3: Left to right. 3 + 16 = 19. Then 19 − 1 = 18. Answer: (A) 18
Problem 43 Puzzle / Logic
If all Bloops are Razzies and all Razzies are Loogies, which must be true?
(A) All Loogies are Bloops   (B) All Razzies are Bloops   (C) All Bloops are Loogies   (D) Some Loogies are Razzies   (E) Both C and D
Topic: Puzzle / Logical Reasoning Bloops ⊂ Razzies ⊂ Loogies. (C) All Bloops are Loogies — TRUE (if every Bloop is a Razzie, and every Razzie is a Loogie, then every Bloop is a Loogie). (D) Some Loogies are Razzies — TRUE (the Razzies are inside Loogies, so at least some Loogies are Razzies). Both C and D must be true. Answer: (E) Both C and D
Problem 44 Sequences
What is the next term in the sequence: 2, 6, 18, 54, ...?
(A) 72   (B) 108   (C) 162   (D) 216   (E) 252
Topic: Sequences & Patterns Check the pattern: 2 × 3 = 6, 6 × 3 = 18, 18 × 3 = 54. Each term is multiplied by 3. Next: 54 × 3 = 162. This is a geometric sequence with ratio 3. Answer: (C) 162
Problem 45 Visual Spatial
A cube is painted red on all faces and then cut into 27 equal smaller cubes. How many small cubes have exactly one face painted red?
(A) 1   (B) 6   (C) 8   (D) 12   (E) 26
Topic: Visual Spatial Reasoning 27 small cubes = 3×3×3 (think Rubik's cube). Two cuts along each direction: left-right, front-back, top-bottom. Each small cube sits in one of four positions: • 8 corner cubes — 3 painted faces each • 12 edge cubes — 2 painted faces each • 6 face-center cubes — 1 painted face each • 1 hidden center cube — 0 painted faces Check: 8 + 12 + 6 + 1 = 27 ✓ The question asks "exactly one face" — that's the 6 face-center cubes, one in the middle of each flat side. Best approach: Memorize the breakdown — 8, 12, 6, 1. If they ask 3 faces = 8. Two faces = 12. One face = 6. Zero = 1. That's all you need. Answer: (B) 6

Formulas to Know Cold

m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)
Slope — how steep is the line? Think of walking on a hill. Slope tells you how much you go up (or down) for every step forward. Subtract the y's on top, the x's on bottom. If the answer is positive, the line goes uphill. Negative means downhill. Zero means flat.
What is the slope of the line passing through (1, 3) and (4, 15)?
(A) 2   (B) 3   (C) 4   (D) 5   (E) 6
Solution
Step 1: Write the two points side by side. (1, 3) and (4, 15) Step 2: Subtract the y's (top of fraction). 15 − 3 = 12 Step 3: Subtract the x's (bottom of fraction). 4 − 1 = 3 Step 4: Divide. 12 ÷ 3 = 4 Trick: Always do y's on top, x's on bottom. If you mix them up, you get the wrong sign.
Answer: (C) 4
y = mx + b
The straight line formula. Every straight line can be written this way. m is the slope (how steep). b is where the line starts when x = 0. In word problems, m is usually a rate ("$30 per month") and b is a one-time fee ("$50 to sign up").
What is the slope of the line y = −2x + 7?
(A) 7   (B) 2   (C) −2   (D) −7   (E) 1/2
Solution
Step 1: Check if it matches y = mx + b. y = −2x + 7 ← yes, it already does Step 2: The slope is the number stuck to the x. That's −2. Step 3: Don't get tricked by the 7. That's the y-intercept (b), not the slope. Trick: If the equation isn't in y = _x + _ form yet, solve for y first. Get y alone on one side.
Answer: (C) −2
a/b = c/d → ad = bc
Cross multiplication — solving fraction equations. When two fractions are equal, draw an X connecting the diagonals and multiply across. It's a shortcut to get rid of both fractions at once. Then solve for the missing number like a normal equation.
If 5/8 = x/24, what is x?
(A) 10   (B) 12   (C) 15   (D) 18   (E) 20
Solution
Step 1: Draw an X between the two fractions. 5/8 = x/24 Connect 5 to 24 (diagonal) and 8 to x (diagonal). Step 2: Multiply each diagonal. 5 × 24 — break this up to make it easier: 5 × 20 = 100, and 5 × 4 = 20. So 5 × 24 = 120. 8 × x = 8x Step 3: Set them equal. 120 = 8x Step 4: Get x alone. Divide both sides by 8. 120 ÷ 8 — break this up too: How many 8's fit in 120? 8 × 10 = 80. Subtract: 120 − 80 = 40. 8 × 5 = 40. That's the rest. So 10 + 5 = 15. x = 15. Multiplication shortcut: When one number is 5, multiply by 10 first and cut in half. 5 × 24: Think 10 × 24 = 240, then half of 240 = 120.
Answer: (C) 15
% = (part / whole) × 100
Finding a percentage — "what slice of the pie?" Put the part you're looking at on top, the whole thing on the bottom, then multiply by 100 to turn it into a percent. Example: you made 18 out of 24 free throws — that's 18/24 = 0.75 = 75%.
A basketball team won 18 out of 24 games. What percentage of games did they win?
(A) 60%   (B) 70%   (C) 75%   (D) 80%   (E) 85%
Solution
Step 1: Write it as a fraction. 18/24 (wins on top, total on bottom) Step 2: Simplify. Start by asking: are both numbers even? 18 → yes (ends in 8). 24 → yes (ends in 4). Divide both by 2: 18 ÷ 2 = 9 24 ÷ 2 = 12 Now you have 9/12. Step 3: Ask again: can I simplify 9/12? Are both even? 9 is odd, so no. Try 3: Does 3 go into 9? Yes → 9 ÷ 3 = 3. Does 3 go into 12? Yes → 12 ÷ 3 = 4. Now you have 3/4. Can't simplify further. Step 4: Convert 3/4 to a percentage. Think money: 1 quarter = 25 cents. 3 quarters = 75 cents = $0.75. Move the decimal 2 places right: 75%. Or if the money trick doesn't click, do the division: 3 ÷ 4: How many times does 4 go into 3? Zero. Write 0. Add a decimal and a zero: 4 goes into 30? Seven times (7 × 4 = 28). Remainder 2. Bring down a zero: 4 goes into 20? Five times exactly. Answer: 0.75, which is 75%. Fractions to know by heart (saves time on test day): 1/4 = 25% 1/2 = 50% 3/4 = 75% 1/3 ≈ 33% 2/3 ≈ 67% 1/5 = 20% 1/10 = 10% 1/8 = 12.5% 3/8 = 37.5%
Answer: (C) 75%
P = favorable / total
Probability — what are the chances? Count the outcomes you want, divide by the total number of outcomes. If there are 6 blue marbles in a bag of 15, the chance of grabbing blue is 6/15. Always simplify the fraction at the end.
A bag has 4 red, 6 blue, and 5 green marbles. If you pick one at random, what is the probability it is blue?
(A) 1/5   (B) 1/3   (C) 2/5   (D) 2/3   (E) 6/10
Solution
Step 1: Count the total. Add all the groups. 4 + 6 = 10. Then 10 + 5 = 15. 15 marbles total. Step 2: Put what you want on top, total on bottom. We want blue. There are 6 blue. P(blue) = 6/15 Step 3: Simplify 6/15. Work through the checklist: Try 2: Are both even? 6 is even, but 15 is odd. No. Try 3: Does 3 go into 6? Yes (6 ÷ 3 = 2). Does 3 go into 15? Yes (15 ÷ 3 = 5). Divide both by 3: 6 ÷ 3 = 2 15 ÷ 3 = 5 Answer: 2/5 Step 4: Scan the answer choices. Look for 2/5. That's (C). How to check if 3 goes into a number: Add the digits. If that sum divides by 3, the number does too. 15 → 1 + 5 = 6. Does 3 go into 6? Yes. So 3 goes into 15. 24 → 2 + 4 = 6. Yes. 17 → 1 + 7 = 8. Does 3 go into 8? No.
Answer: (C) 2/5
P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
The "NOT" shortcut. If you know the chance of something happening, subtract it from 1 to get the chance of it NOT happening. Think pizza: if 1 out of 4 slices is pepperoni, then 3 out of 4 are NOT pepperoni. 1 − 1/4 = 3/4.
The probability of picking a red card from a deck is 1/4. What is the probability of NOT picking a red card?
(A) 1/4   (B) 1/2   (C) 3/4   (D) 1/3   (E) 2/3
Solution
Step 1: Write down what you know. P(red) = 1/4 Step 2: Think about it like a pizza. The whole pizza = 1 (all of it). Red takes up 1 out of 4 slices. "Not red" is the leftover slices. Step 3: Subtract. But you can't do 1 − 1/4 directly. Turn the 1 into a fraction with the same bottom number. The bottom number is 4, so write 1 as 4/4. (4/4 = 1, just like 4 quarters = 1 dollar.) Step 4: Now subtract normally. 4/4 − 1/4 = 3/4 Same bottom, subtract the tops: 4 − 1 = 3. Shortcut for next time: When you see "1 minus a fraction," just ask: "How many slices are left?" 1 − 1/4: 4 slices total, take away 1, that's 3 left → 3/4. 1 − 2/5: 5 slices total, take away 2, that's 3 left → 3/5. 1 − 3/10: 10 slices total, take away 3, that's 7 left → 7/10.
Answer: (C) 3/4
d = √[(x₂-x₁)²+(y₂-y₁)²]
How far apart are two points? Find the horizontal gap (subtract the x's) and the vertical gap (subtract the y's). Square both, add them, take the square root. It's just the Pythagorean theorem in disguise. Shortcut: if you see gaps of 3 and 4, the distance is 5. Gaps of 6 and 8 → distance is 10.
What is the distance between points (2, 1) and (5, 5)?
(A) 3   (B) 4   (C) 5   (D) 6   (E) 7
Solution
Step 1: Line up your points. Point 1: (2, 1) Point 2: (5, 5) Step 2: Subtract the x's. 5 − 2 = 3. Write "3" off to the side. Step 3: Subtract the y's. 5 − 1 = 4. Write "4" off to the side. Step 4: Square each one. ("Squared" means multiply it by itself.) 3² = 3 × 3 = 9 4² = 4 × 4 = 16 Step 5: Add those two results. 9 + 16 = 25 Step 6: Take the square root. Ask: "What number times itself = 25?" 5 × 5 = 25. So √25 = 5. Squares to memorize (you will need these): 1²=1 2²=4 3²=9 4²=16 5²=25 6²=36 7²=49 8²=64 9²=81 10²=100 11²=121 12²=144 If you know these, you can work backwards to find square roots instantly.
Answer: (C) 5
mid = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Finding the exact middle between two points. Add the two x-values and divide by 2. Add the two y-values and divide by 2. You're just averaging each coordinate separately — same thing you'd do to find the average of two test scores.
What is the midpoint between (3, 7) and (9, 1)?
(A) (6, 4)   (B) (5, 3)   (C) (6, 3)   (D) (12, 8)   (E) (3, 4)
Solution
Step 1: Add the two x-values. 3 + 9 = 12 Step 2: Divide by 2 (find the average). 12 ÷ 2 = 6 ← that's your x Step 3: Add the two y-values. 7 + 1 = 8 Step 4: Divide by 2. 8 ÷ 2 = 4 ← that's your y Step 5: Write the midpoint. (6, 4) Trick: Midpoint = average. You're just finding the middle of each coordinate separately. Same thing you'd do to find the average of two test scores.
Answer: (A) (6, 4)
a² - b² = (a+b)(a-b)
A shortcut for factoring. When you see something squared minus a number, check if that number is a perfect square (4, 9, 16, 25, 36...). If it is, you can split it into two pieces instantly: one with a plus and one with a minus. x² − 36 = (x+6)(x−6). No guessing needed.
Factor: x² − 36
(A) (x+6)(x+6)   (B) (x−6)(x−6)   (C) (x+6)(x−6)   (D) (x+9)(x−4)   (E) (x+12)(x−3)
Solution
Step 1: Check — is this "something² minus something²"? x² → yes, that's x squared. 36 → is 36 a perfect square? 6 × 6 = 36. Yes. Step 2: Identify a and b. a = x (the thing being squared on the left) b = 6 (the thing being squared on the right) Step 3: Plug into the pattern (a+b)(a−b). = (x + 6)(x − 6) Step 4: Quick check — FOIL it back out. x×x = x², x×(−6) = −6x, 6×x = 6x, 6×(−6) = −36 The −6x and +6x cancel out → x² − 36 ✓ Trick: If you see [something] − [a number], check if that number is a perfect square (4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64...). If yes, this pattern works.
Answer: (C) (x+6)(x−6)
