Know the Test
How the SSAT Works

Upper Level. 5 scored sections + 1 unscored writing sample. About 3 hours total.

📋 The Sections (in order)

  1. Writing Sample — 25 min. Pick one of two prompts, write an essay. Not scored, but GAC will see it.
  2. ⏱ 10-minute break — snack, restroom
  3. Quantitative (Math) 1 — 30 min, 25 questions
  4. Reading Comprehension — 40 min, 40 questions
  5. ⏱ 10-minute break
  6. Verbal — 30 min, 60 questions. Synonyms (30) + analogies (30).
  7. Quantitative (Math) 2 — 30 min, 25 questions
  8. Experimental — 15 min, 16 questions. Not scored. Don't stress.

🎯 Scoring

  1. Each scored section: 440–710 points. Total score range: 1320–2130.
  2. +1 point for each correct answer.
  3. −¼ point for each wrong answer. Blank = no penalty.
  4. Your percentile is what matters most — it compares you to other test-takers. GAC looks for above 50th percentile.

Test Day Strategy

Smart play beats hard play. These rules are worth points.

🧠 The 4 Rules

  1. Eliminate then guess. If you can cross off even 1 answer, guess from the rest. If you have zero clue, skip it — the ¼-point penalty means random guessing hurts you.
  2. Don't get stuck. Every question is worth the same. If one is taking too long, mark it, skip, come back. Keep moving.
  3. Easy ones first. Skim the section, answer what you know, then go back for the harder ones. Bank those points.
  4. Watch the clock. Verbal is fast — 60 questions in 30 minutes = 30 seconds each. Don't overthink synonyms, go with your gut.

✍️ Writing Sample Tips

  1. Pick the prompt you have a real opinion on. Don't pick the "harder" one to impress anyone.
  2. Intro → 2-3 body paragraphs with examples → conclusion. Keep it organized.
  3. Schools see this. Write in your own voice, not like a robot. Be clear, not fancy.

🏁 Morning Of

  1. Eat a real breakfast. Not just a granola bar.
  2. Bring: admission ticket (printed or on phone), 3 sharpened #2 pencils with good erasers, snacks in a clear bag, drink in a clear bottle with your name on it.
  3. Don't bring: watches (none allowed — clocks are in the room), calculator, backpack, mechanical pencils, pens, or notes. Phone must be OFF and goes in a zip bag at the door.
  4. Arrive 30-45 min early. Late = no entry once testing starts.
  5. You've done the work. Stay calm, trust your prep.

Formulas to Know Cold

m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)
Slope. Subtract the y-values on top, x-values on bottom. Tells you how steep the line is. Positive = goes up, negative = goes down.
y = mx + b
Slope-intercept form. m is the slope, b is where the line crosses the y-axis. If they give you an equation like y = 3x + 5, the slope is 3.
a/b = c/d → ad = bc
Cross multiplication. When two fractions are equal, cross multiply to solve for the unknown. Example: 3/4 = x/20 → 60 = 4x → x = 15.
% = (part / whole) × 100
Finding a percentage. Divide the part by the whole, multiply by 100. Example: 15 out of 60 = (15/60)×100 = 25%.
P = favorable / total
Probability. Count what you want, divide by everything possible. 3 red marbles out of 12 total = 3/12 = 1/4.
P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
Probability of something NOT happening. Subtract from 1. If there's a 30% chance of rain, there's a 70% chance of no rain.
d = √[(x₂-x₁)²+(y₂-y₁)²]
Distance between two points. Subtract the x's, subtract the y's, square both, add them, take the square root. It's just the Pythagorean theorem on a graph.
mid = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Midpoint. Average the two x-values, average the two y-values. That gives you the point exactly in the middle.
a² - b² = (a+b)(a-b)
Difference of squares. When you see one perfect square minus another, it factors into two binomials. Example: x² - 25 = (x+5)(x-5).
