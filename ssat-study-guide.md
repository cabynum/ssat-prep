## Flashcards — Math Topics

### SLOPE

**Card 1 — What is slope?**
Slope measures how steep a line is. It's the ratio of vertical change to horizontal change.
> Slope = rise / run

**Card 2 — Slope formula**
Given two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂):
> m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)

**Card 3 — Example**
Find the slope between (2, 3) and (6, 11):
> m = (11 - 3) / (6 - 2) = 8 / 4 = 2

**Card 4 — Positive vs. negative slope**
- Positive slope: line goes UP from left to right ↗
- Negative slope: line goes DOWN from left to right ↘
- Zero slope: horizontal line →
- Undefined slope: vertical line ↑

**Card 5 — Slope-intercept form**
> y = mx + b
- m = slope
- b = y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)

**Card 6 — Parallel and perpendicular lines**
- Parallel lines have the SAME slope
- Perpendicular lines have slopes that are NEGATIVE RECIPROCALS (flip and change the sign)
- Example: if slope = 2/3, perpendicular slope = -3/2

---

### RATES, RATIOS, PROPORTIONS, PERCENTAGES

**Card 7 — What is a ratio?**
A ratio compares two quantities.
> 3 apples to 5 oranges = 3:5 = 3/5

**Card 8 — What is a proportion?**
A proportion says two ratios are equal.
> a/b = c/d

**Card 9 — Cross multiplication**
To solve a proportion, cross multiply:
> a/b = c/d → a × d = b × c

**Card 10 — Example**
If 3/4 = x/20, find x:
> 3 × 20 = 4 × x
> 60 = 4x
> x = 15

**Card 11 — Rate problems**
A rate compares two quantities with different units.
> 150 miles in 3 hours = 150/3 = 50 miles per hour
Find the rate per ONE unit first, then multiply.

**Card 12 — Percentage formula**
> Percentage = (part / whole) × 100

**Card 13 — Finding percent of a number**
What is 25% of 80?
> 0.25 × 80 = 20

**Card 14 — Percent change**
> Percent change = (new - original) / original × 100

---

### COORDINATE GEOMETRY

**Card 15 — The coordinate plane**
- Horizontal line = x-axis
- Vertical line = y-axis
- They cross at the origin (0, 0)
- A point is written as (x, y) — x first (left/right), y second (up/down)

**Card 16 — Four quadrants**
- Quadrant I: (+, +) — upper right
- Quadrant II: (-, +) — upper left
- Quadrant III: (-, -) — lower left
- Quadrant IV: (+, -) — lower right

**Card 17 — Distance between two points**
> d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]

**Card 18 — Example**
Distance between (1, 2) and (4, 6):
> d = √[(4-1)² + (6-2)²] = √[9 + 16] = √25 = 5

**Card 19 — Midpoint formula**
The midpoint between (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂):
> ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)

**Card 20 — Example**
Midpoint between (2, 4) and (8, 10):
> ((2+8)/2, (4+10)/2) = (5, 7)

---

### PROBABILITY

**Card 21 — Basic probability formula**
> P(event) = number of favorable outcomes / total number of possible outcomes

**Card 22 — Example**
A bag has 3 red, 4 blue, and 5 green marbles. What's the probability of picking a red one?
> P(red) = 3 / (3+4+5) = 3/12 = 1/4

**Card 23 — Probability range**
- Probability is always between 0 and 1
- 0 = impossible
- 1 = certain
- 0.5 = equally likely / unlikely

**Card 24 — Probability of NOT an event**
> P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
If P(rain) = 0.3, then P(no rain) = 0.7

**Card 25 — Independent events**
When two events don't affect each other, MULTIPLY their probabilities.
> P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
Example: flip a coin AND roll a 6 = 1/2 × 1/6 = 1/12

**Card 26 — Counting outcomes**
If you have 3 shirts and 4 pants, how many outfits?
> 3 × 4 = 12 outfits

---

### FACTORING

**Card 27 — What is factoring?**
Breaking an expression into pieces (factors) that multiply together to give the original expression. It's the reverse of distributing.

**Card 28 — Greatest Common Factor (GCF)**
Find the largest number/variable that divides into every term.
> 6x + 9 → GCF is 3 → 3(2x + 3)

**Card 29 — Example with variables**
> 4x² + 8x → GCF is 4x → 4x(x + 2)

**Card 30 — Difference of squares**
> a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)
Example: x² - 25 = (x + 5)(x - 5)

**Card 31 — Factoring trinomials (x² + bx + c)**
Find two numbers that MULTIPLY to c and ADD to b.
> x² + 7x + 12 → numbers: 3 and 4 (3×4=12, 3+4=7)
> = (x + 3)(x + 4)

**Card 32 — Check your work**
Always multiply your factors back out (FOIL) to verify you get the original expression.
