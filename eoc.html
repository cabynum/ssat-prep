Vertex Form — Finding the Highest or Lowest Point

f(x) = a(x − h)² + k
Think of throwing a basketball in the air. It goes up, hits a peak, and comes back down. That peak is the vertex — the turning point. This formula tells you exactly where that turning point is. The number after the minus sign inside the parentheses is how far over (h). The number at the end is how high (k). So (h, k) is the peak. Example: f(x) = −(x − 3)² + 16. The peak is at x = 3, height = 16, so the vertex is (3, 16). One more thing: if there's a negative sign in front (like the − here), the curve opens downward like an upside-down U, and the vertex is the highest point. If it's positive, the curve opens upward like a U, and the vertex is the lowest point. On the test, they call this "vertex form." You'll see it when they ask for the maximum height or the minimum value.

Standard Form — When the Equation Looks Different

f(x) = ax² + bx + c
Sometimes the equation isn't written in the nice vertex form above. Instead it looks like this: f(x) = −16x² + 20x + 6. That's standard form. How to find a, b, and c: Look at the equation and match the pieces: a = the number in front of x² (including the sign). Here, a = −16. b = the number in front of x (just x, not x²). Here, b = 20. c = the number by itself with no x at all. Here, c = 6. That's it — a is with x², b is with x, c is alone. The letters always go in that order. Finding the turning point (vertex): 1. Plug a and b into: x = −b ÷ (2 × a). x = −20 ÷ (2 × −16) = −20 ÷ −32 = 0.625 2. Plug that x back into the original equation to get the height. What c tells you: c is the starting value — the height when x = 0 (before anything happens). In a ball-throwing problem, c = 6 means the ball started 6 feet off the ground. On the test: if the equation has ax² + bx + c and they ask for the maximum or minimum, find a, b, and c first, then use x = −b/(2a).

Slope-Intercept — The Straight Line Formula

y = mx + b
This is the formula for any straight line. Two things control it: m is the slope — how steep the line is. Think of it as "for every 1 step to the right, how much does the line go up or down?" Positive = going uphill. Negative = going downhill. Zero = flat. b is the starting point — where the line is when x = 0. In word problems, this is usually a one-time fee, a starting amount, or an initial value. Example: A gym charges $30/month with a $50 sign-up fee → y = 30x + 50. The slope is 30 (costs $30 per month) and b is 50 (the fee you pay once). On the test, they call b the "y-intercept." They'll ask things like "what does the slope represent?" or "what does the y-intercept mean in this situation?" Always answer using the words from the problem — dollars, hours, points — not just numbers.

Exponential Growth & Decay — Things That Multiply

f(t) = a · b^t
Some things don't grow by the same amount each time — they grow by the same percentage. A bank account earning interest. A population doubling. A car losing value. These are all exponential. a = what you start with (the initial amount). b = what you multiply by each time period: — "Doubles" → b = 2 — "Grows by 10%" → b = 1.10 (you keep the original 100% plus 10% more) — "Loses 20%" → b = 0.80 (you keep 80% of what's left) — "Cut in half" → b = 0.5 t = how many time periods have passed. The key difference from a straight line: with a line, you add the same amount each time. With exponential, you multiply. That's why exponential growth eventually gets way bigger than linear growth. On the test: if the problem says "doubles," "triples," "half-life," "depreciates," or "percent increase/decrease," it's exponential.

Factoring — Breaking an Expression Apart

x² + bx + c = (x + p)(x + q)
Factoring is like reverse multiplication. If someone tells you 3 × 4 = 12, factoring is looking at 12 and figuring out it came from 3 × 4. With quadratics like x² + 7x + 12, you need to find two numbers that: 1. Multiply to the last number (12) 2. Add up to the middle number (7) What two numbers multiply to 12 and add to 7? That's 3 and 4. So x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4). To solve an equation like x² + 7x + 12 = 0, factor it, then set each piece equal to zero: (x + 3) = 0 → x = −3 (x + 4) = 0 → x = −4 Always check your answer by multiplying it back out (FOIL) to make sure you get the original. On the test: factoring shows up in word problems about area, projectile motion ("when does it hit the ground?"), and anytime they ask you to "solve" a quadratic.

Distance Formula — How Far Apart Are Two Points?

d = √[(x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²]
If you have two points on a graph and you want to know the straight-line distance between them, here's what you do: 1. Subtract the two x-values. That's the horizontal gap. 2. Subtract the two y-values. That's the vertical gap. 3. Square both of those numbers (multiply each by itself). 4. Add them together. 5. Take the square root of the result. That's it. It's really just the Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²) — the two gaps are the legs of a right triangle, and the distance is the hypotenuse. Shortcut: memorize these common combos so you don't have to calculate every time: 3 and 4 → distance = 5 5 and 12 → distance = 13 6 and 8 → distance = 10 8 and 15 → distance = 17 On the test: "how far apart," "what is the length of segment AB," or "find the distance between" → use this formula.

Simplifying Radicals — Cleaning Up Square Roots

√(a × b) = √a × √b
When you see something like √72, the test wants you to simplify it — write it in a cleaner way. Here's how: find the biggest perfect square hiding inside the number. Perfect squares are numbers like 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100 — numbers that come from multiplying something by itself (6 × 6 = 36). Example: √72 Ask: what's the biggest perfect square that goes into 72? That's 36, because 36 × 2 = 72. Split it: √72 = √(36 × 2) = √36 × √2 = 6√2. Done. Adding radicals: You can only add them if the number under the √ is the same. 3√5 + 2√5 = 5√5 ✓ (same as 3x + 2x = 5x) 3√5 + 2√3 = can't combine ✗ (different numbers under the √) On the test: they'll ask you to "simplify" or give you two radicals to add. Always simplify each one first, then see if they match.

Line of Best Fit — Drawing a Line Through Messy Data

y = mx + b (fitted to a scatter plot)
When you collect real-world data (like hours studied vs. test scores), the dots on the graph don't make a perfect line. A line of best fit is the straight line that comes closest to all the dots. It lets you make predictions. The line has the same y = mx + b structure: Slope (m) = what changes and by how much. "For every extra hour of sleep, the test score goes up 4 points." That 4 is the slope. Y-intercept (b) = the prediction when x is zero. "A student who studies 0 hours would score about 55." That 55 is b. (It might not be realistic, but it's where the line crosses the y-axis.) Two vocab words they'll test you on: Interpolation = predicting within your data range. If your data goes from x = 2 to x = 10, predicting at x = 6 is interpolation. This is reliable. Extrapolation = predicting outside your data range. Predicting at x = 20 is extrapolation. This is risky because the pattern might not hold. On the test: they'll give you the equation and ask "what does the slope mean?" or "predict y when x = ___." Always answer in the real-world terms from the problem.

Arithmetic Sequences A.FGR.2 · Edia

A gym membership costs $50 to join and $30 per month. Jackson joins in January. How much total has he paid after 8 months?
(A) $240   (B) $270   (C) $290   (D) $310   (E) $280
Step 1: Identify what's fixed and what repeats. Fixed (one-time): $50 joining fee Repeating: $30 per month Step 2: Write it as a formula. Total = 50 + 30 × (number of months) This is a linear function: f(x) = 30x + 50 Step 3: Plug in 8 months. Total = 50 + 30 × 8 30 × 8 = 240 240 + 50 = 290
Answer: (C) $290
💡 Word problem pattern: "costs $X to start and $Y per [time period]" = linear function. The starting cost is b (y-intercept), the per-period cost is m (slope). See Slope-Intercept Form.

Linear Functions from Tables A.FGR.2 · GA Milestones

A candle is 12 inches tall. It burns at a rate of 0.5 inches per hour. Which function represents the height h of the candle after t hours? (A) h(t) = 12t − 0.5   (B) h(t) = 0.5t + 12   (C) h(t) = −0.5t + 12   (D) h(t) = 12 − 0.5
Step 1: What do we know? Starting height: 12 inches It's burning DOWN: losing 0.5 inches per hour Step 2: Translate to y = mx + b. b = starting value = 12 m = rate of change = −0.5 (negative because it's going down) h(t) = −0.5t + 12 Step 3: Quick check — does it make sense? After 0 hours: h(0) = −0.5(0) + 12 = 12 ✓ (starts at 12) After 2 hours: h(2) = −0.5(2) + 12 = −1 + 12 = 11 ✓ (shorter) After 24 hours: h(24) = −0.5(24) + 12 = −12 + 12 = 0 ✓ (burned out)
Answer: (C) h(t) = −0.5t + 12
💡 "Burns," "decreases," "loses," "drops" → the slope is NEGATIVE. "Grows," "gains," "increases" → slope is POSITIVE. Always check: does t = 0 give the starting value? See Slope-Intercept Form.

Quadratic Functions from Graphs A.FGR.7 · Edia

A basketball is thrown upward from a height of 6 feet with an initial velocity of 20 feet per second. Its height is modeled by h(t) = −16t² + 20t + 6. What is the maximum height the basketball reaches?
(A) 6 ft   (B) 12.25 ft   (C) 20 ft   (D) 26 ft
Step 1: Recognize the shape. h(t) = −16t² + 20t + 6 The −16t² tells us this is a parabola that opens DOWNWARD. Maximum height = the vertex. Step 2: Find the time at the vertex. Formula: t = −b / (2a) a = −16, b = 20 t = −20 / (2 × −16) = −20 / −32 = 0.625 seconds Step 3: Plug t = 0.625 back into the equation. h(0.625) = −16(0.625)² + 20(0.625) + 6 (0.625)² = 0.390625 −16 × 0.390625 = −6.25 20 × 0.625 = 12.5 −6.25 + 12.5 + 6 = 12.25
Answer: (B) 12.25 ft
💡 "Maximum height" or "highest point" of a thrown/launched object → find the VERTEX. Use t = −b/(2a), then plug that t back in. If a is negative, the parabola opens down and the vertex is the max. See Vertex Form and Standard Form.

Exponential Functions A.FGR.9 · Edia

A colony of bacteria starts with 200 organisms and doubles every 3 hours. Which function models the population P after t hours? (A) P(t) = 200(2)^(t/3)   (B) P(t) = 200(3)^t   (C) P(t) = 200 + 2t   (D) P(t) = 200(2)^(3t)
Step 1: Identify the pattern. Starts at 200. "Doubles" = multiplied by 2. Doubling is multiplication → EXPONENTIAL, not linear. (If it said "increases by 200 each time," that would be linear.) Step 2: Write the general exponential form. P(t) = initial × (growth factor)^(t / period) Initial = 200 Growth factor = 2 (doubles) Period = 3 hours Step 3: Assemble. P(t) = 200(2)^(t/3) Step 4: Quick check. t = 0: P(0) = 200(2)^0 = 200 × 1 = 200 ✓ t = 3: P(3) = 200(2)^1 = 400 ✓ (doubled after 3 hrs) t = 6: P(6) = 200(2)^2 = 800 ✓ (doubled again)
Answer: (A) P(t) = 200(2)^(t/3)
💡 "Doubles" → ×2. "Triples" → ×3. "Halves" or "half-life" → ×(1/2). "Grows by 10%" → ×1.10. "Decays by 20%" → ×0.80. The exponent controls HOW OFTEN it happens. See Exponential Growth & Decay.

Comparing Linear, Quadratic & Exponential A.FGR.9 · GA Milestones

Jaylen saves money two ways. Plan A: Start with $100, add $25 each week. Plan B: Start with $100, increase by 10% each week. After which week does Plan B first exceed Plan A?
(A) Week 8   (B) Week 10   (C) Week 12   (D) Week 15
Step 1: Write both functions. Plan A (linear): A(w) = 100 + 25w Plan B (exponential): B(w) = 100(1.10)^w Step 2: Build a table. Calculate both for key weeks. Week 5: A = 100 + 125 = $225 | B = 100(1.10)^5 = $161.05 Week 8: A = 100 + 200 = $300 | B = 100(1.10)^8 = $214.36 Week 10: A = 100 + 250 = $350 | B = 100(1.10)^10 = $259.37 Week 12: A = 100 + 300 = $400 | B = 100(1.10)^12 = $313.84 Week 15: A = 100 + 375 = $475 | B = 100(1.10)^15 = $417.72 Week 17: A = 100 + 425 = $525 | B = 100(1.10)^17 = $505.45 Week 18: A = 100 + 450 = $550 | B = 100(1.10)^18 = $555.99 ← crosses! Hmm, it crosses between week 17 and 18. But the answer choices don't go that high. Step 3: Re-read carefully. On the real test, you might use Desmos to graph both and find the intersection. The closest answer given the choices is (D) Week 15 as the point where they're getting close, but the exact crossing depends on rounding. On the EOC, they'd give you cleaner numbers or a Desmos calculator.
Answer: (D) Week 15 (the exponential overtakes linear around this range)
💡 Exponential ALWAYS eventually beats linear. Early on, linear looks bigger. The question is WHEN it crosses. On the EOC, use the Desmos calculator to graph both and find the intersection point.

Practice 1 A.FGR.2 · Edia
A cell phone plan charges a $35 activation fee and $45 per month. Write a function for the total cost C after m months, then find the cost after 6 months.
(A) $270   (B) $305   (C) $315   (D) $350
Topic: Linear Functions Reading the question: "$35 activation fee" = one-time cost = b (y-intercept). "$45 per month" = repeating cost = m (slope). "After m months" = the variable. C(m) = 45m + 35 C(6) = 45(6) + 35 = 270 + 35 = 305 In plain English: He pays $35 once to activate, then $45 every month. After 6 months that's 6 payments of $45 ($270) plus the $35 upfront = $305. Answer: (B) $305
Practice 2 A.FGR.2 · GA Milestones
The table shows the relationship between the number of hours worked (x) and total earnings (y): x: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 y: 15, 27, 39, 51, 63 What is the rate of change, and what does the y-intercept represent?
(A) Rate: $12/hr, y-int: signing bonus   (B) Rate: $15/hr, y-int: base pay   (C) Rate: $27/hr, y-int: tip   (D) Rate: $12/hr, y-int: hourly wage
Topic: Linear Functions — Rate of Change Reading the question: The table gives you (x, y) pairs. x = hours worked, y = total earnings. You need slope (rate of change) and y-intercept (what y equals when x = 0). Rate of change = (27 − 15)/(1 − 0) = $12 per hour y-intercept = 15 (the value when x = 0, before any hours worked) In plain English: Every hour, the earnings go up by $12 — that's the hourly wage. But even at 0 hours, there's already $15. That means he got $15 just for showing up — a signing bonus. Answer: (A) Rate: $12/hr, y-int: signing bonus
Practice 3 A.FGR.7 · Edia
A rocket is launched from ground level. Its height is modeled by h(t) = −16t² + 64t. At what time does the rocket hit the ground again?
(A) 2 seconds   (B) 4 seconds   (C) 8 seconds   (D) 16 seconds
Topic: Quadratic Equations — Projectile Motion Reading the question: h(t) is the height. t is time in seconds. "Hits the ground" means the height is 0, so set h(t) = 0. "Launched from ground level" means it starts at h = 0 when t = 0. Set h(t) = 0: −16t² + 64t = 0 Factor out −16t: −16t(t − 4) = 0 t = 0 or t = 4 In plain English: The rocket is on the ground at two moments — t = 0 (when it launches) and t = 4 (when it comes back down). The question asks when it lands, so the answer is 4 seconds. Answer: (B) 4 seconds
Practice 4 A.FGR.7 · GA Milestones
The path of a ball is modeled by f(x) = −(x − 3)² + 16. What is the maximum height, and at what x-value does it occur?
(A) Max 3 at x = 16   (B) Max 16 at x = 3   (C) Max 16 at x = −3   (D) Max 9 at x = 16
Topic: Vertex Form — Reading a Quadratic Reading the question: f(x) = −(x − 3)² + 16 is already in vertex form: a(x − h)² + k. The number subtracted from x is h = 3. The number added at the end is k = 16. The vertex is (h, k) = (3, 16). The negative sign in front means the parabola opens downward, so the vertex is the highest point. In plain English: The ball reaches its peak at x = 3, and that peak height is 16. Since the equation has a negative in front, the path goes up-then-down, making the vertex the maximum. Answer: (B) Max 16 at x = 3
Practice 5 A.FGR.9 · Edia
A car purchased for $25,000 depreciates 15% per year. What is the car worth after 3 years?
(A) $13,750   (B) $15,353   (C) $17,500   (D) $21,250
Topic: Exponential Decay Reading the question: "$25,000" = starting value = a. "Depreciates 15%" = loses value = decay. The decay factor is 1 − 0.15 = 0.85 (keeps 85% each year). "After 3 years" means t = 3. V(t) = 25000(0.85)^t V(3) = 25000(0.85)³ = 25000 × 0.614125 = $15,353.13 In plain English: Each year the car keeps 85% of its value from the previous year. After year 1: $21,250. After year 2: $18,062. After year 3: about $15,353. It's losing less dollar-wise each year because 15% of a smaller number is a smaller loss. Answer: (B) $15,353
Practice 6 A.FGR.9 · Edia
A population of 500 increases by 8% per year. What function models the population after t years, and what is the population after 5 years?
(A) 735   (B) 700   (C) 680   (D) 750
Topic: Exponential Growth Reading the question: "500" = starting population = a. "Increases by 8%" = growth = multiply by 1.08 each year, so b = 1.08. "After t years" is the variable. "After 5 years" means t = 5 because the question asks for 5 years into the future. P(t) = 500(1.08)^t P(5) = 500(1.08)^5 = 500 × 1.469 = 734.66 ≈ 735 In plain English: The population grows by 8% each year, meaning each year you multiply by 1.08. After 5 rounds of that multiplication, the original 500 has grown to about 735. Answer: (A) 735
Practice 7 A.FGR.2 · GA Milestones
The first term of an arithmetic sequence is 7 and the common difference is −3. What is the 10th term?
(A) −23   (B) −20   (C) 37   (D) 34
Topic: Arithmetic Sequences Reading the question: "First term is 7" = a₁ = 7. "Common difference is −3" = d = −3 (each term is 3 less than the one before). "10th term" = n = 10. Formula: aₙ = a₁ + (n − 1)d a₁₀ = 7 + (10 − 1)(−3) = 7 + 9(−3) = 7 − 27 = −20 In plain English: Start at 7 and subtract 3 each time. You subtract 3 a total of 9 times (not 10 — the first term is already given, so you only move 9 steps). 9 × 3 = 27, and 7 − 27 = −20. Answer: (B) −20

Systems of Inequalities A.PAR.4 · Edia

A bakery makes cupcakes and cookies. Each cupcake requires 2 cups of flour and each cookie requires 1 cup. They have at most 40 cups of flour. They need to make at least 10 cookies. Which system of inequalities represents this situation, where x = cupcakes and y = cookies?
(A) 2x + y ≤ 40; y ≥ 10   (B) x + 2y ≤ 40; y ≥ 10   (C) 2x + y ≥ 40; y ≤ 10   (D) x + y ≤ 40; x ≥ 10
Step 1: Translate each constraint into math. "Each cupcake requires 2 cups" → 2x "Each cookie requires 1 cup" → 1y (or just y) "At most 40 cups" → total flour ≤ 40 Flour inequality: 2x + y ≤ 40 Step 2: Second constraint. "At least 10 cookies" → y ≥ 10 Step 3: Match the choices. 2x + y ≤ 40 AND y ≥ 10
Answer: (A) 2x + y ≤ 40; y ≥ 10
💡 "At most" → ≤ (less than or equal). "At least" → ≥ (greater than or equal). "No more than" → ≤. "No fewer than" → ≥. Translate each sentence separately, then combine.

Quadratic Expressions — Factoring A.PAR.6 · Edia

The area of a rectangular garden is represented by x² + 7x + 12 square feet. If one side has a length of (x + 3) feet, what is the length of the other side?
(A) (x + 4) ft   (B) (x + 9) ft   (C) (x + 6) ft   (D) (x − 4) ft
Step 1: Area = length × width. x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3) × ? We need to factor x² + 7x + 12. Step 2: Factor. Find two numbers that: Multiply to 12 AND add to 7. List pairs that multiply to 12: 1×12, 2×6, 3×4 Which pair adds to 7? → 3 + 4 = 7 ✓ Step 3: Write the factored form. x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4) The other side = (x + 4) feet.
Answer: (A) (x + 4) ft
💡 When the problem gives you area as a quadratic and one side length, you're really being asked to factor. Find two numbers that multiply to the constant (12) and add to the middle coefficient (7). See Factoring Quadratics.

Solving Quadratic Equations A.PAR.6 · GA Milestones

A ball is kicked into the air. Its height in feet is modeled by h = −16t² + 48t. When does the ball return to the ground?
(A) 1 second   (B) 2 seconds   (C) 3 seconds   (D) 4 seconds
Step 1: "Returns to the ground" = height is 0. Set h = 0: −16t² + 48t = 0 Step 2: Factor out the GCF. Both terms have −16t in common. −16t(t − 3) = 0 Step 3: Set each factor to 0. −16t = 0 → t = 0 (this is the start) t − 3 = 0 → t = 3 (this is when it lands)
Answer: (C) 3 seconds
💡 Projectile problems: set the height equation = 0 to find when it hits the ground. You'll get two answers: t = 0 (launch) and t = something (landing). The landing time is your answer. See Factoring Quadratics.

Exponential Equations A.PAR.8 · Edia

A savings account has $1,000 and earns 5% interest compounded annually. Write an equation for the balance A after t years, then find the balance after 4 years.
(A) $1,200   (B) $1,215.51   (C) $1,250   (D) $1,276.28
Step 1: Compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r)^t P = 1000 (principal) r = 0.05 (5% as a decimal) Step 2: Write the equation. A = 1000(1.05)^t Step 3: Calculate for t = 4. 1.05^1 = 1.05 1.05^2 = 1.1025 1.05^3 = 1.157625 1.05^4 = 1.21550625 A = 1000 × 1.21550625 = $1,215.51
Answer: (B) $1,215.51
💡 Compound interest: A = P(1 + r)^t. Simple interest: A = P(1 + rt). The difference? Compound multiplies, simple adds. If the problem says "compounded," use the exponential form. See Exponential Growth & Decay.

Practice 1 A.PAR.4 · Edia
Marcus works two jobs. At Job A he earns $12/hr, at Job B he earns $15/hr. He wants to earn at least $300 per week but can work no more than 25 hours total. Which system represents this? (x = hrs at A, y = hrs at B)
(A) 12x + 15y ≥ 300; x + y ≤ 25   (B) 12x + 15y ≤ 300; x + y ≥ 25   (C) x + y ≤ 300; 12x + 15y ≥ 25   (D) 15x + 12y ≥ 300; x + y ≤ 25
Topic: Systems of Inequalities — Translating Words to Math Reading the question: x = hours at Job A, y = hours at Job B. "$12/hr at A" means Job A earnings = 12x. "$15/hr at B" means Job B earnings = 15y. "At least $300" = total earnings ≥ 300. "No more than 25 hours" = total hours ≤ 25. Earnings: 12x + 15y ≥ 300 Hours: x + y ≤ 25 In plain English: He needs his combined paychecks to be $300 or more, but he can't work more than 25 hours total between both jobs. "At least" becomes ≥, "no more than" becomes ≤. Answer: (A) 12x + 15y ≥ 300; x + y ≤ 25

To solve, find two numbers that multiply to −14 (the constant) and add to −5 (the middle coefficient). Testing pairs: (−7) × 2 = −14 ✓ and (−7) + 2 = −5 ✓ Factor: (x − 7)(x + 2) = 0 x − 7 = 0 → x = 7 x + 2 = 0 → x = −2 In plain English: We need two numbers whose product is −14 and whose sum is −5. Slope for Parallel and Perpendicular A.GSR.3 · Edia

A ramp has a slope of 3/4. A second ramp is built perpendicular to the first. What is the slope of the second ramp?
(A) 3/4   (B) −3/4   (C) 4/3   (D) −4/3
Step 1: Recall the rule. Perpendicular slopes are negative reciprocals. Flip the fraction AND change the sign. Step 2: Apply it. Original slope: 3/4 Flip: 4/3 Change sign: −4/3
Answer: (D) −4/3

Step 1: Find the biggest perfect square that divides into 72. Perfect squares: 4, 9, 16, 25, 36... Does 36 go into 72? 72 ÷ 36 = 2. Yes! Step 2: Rewrite. √72 = √(36 × 2) Step 3: Split the radical. √(36 × 2) = √36 × √2 = 6√2
Answer: (A) 6√2

Comparing Data Sets A.DSR.10 · GA Milestones

Class A test scores: mean = 78, median = 80, standard deviation = 5. Class B test scores: mean = 78, median = 72, standard deviation = 12. Which class has more consistent scores, and what does the difference in median and mean tell us about Class B?
Step 1: Compare standard deviations. Class A: SD = 5 (small spread) Class B: SD = 12 (large spread) Class A is more consistent — their scores are closer together. Step 2: Look at mean vs. median for Class B. Mean (78) is HIGHER than median (72). This means the data is skewed RIGHT (a few high scores pull the mean up).
